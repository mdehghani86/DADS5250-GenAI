Every once in a while, a technology comes along that changes everything.

In November 2022, OpenAI released ChatGPT. And since then, the way we do things has never been the same.

We no longer write code the way we used to. We no longer create presentations the way we used to. The way we analyze data, generate images, build applications -- all of it, fundamentally different.

A code writer. A slide designer. A data analyst. An image creator. A research assistant. Not five separate tools. One technology. Generative AI.

And this course is designed to show you how engineering problems -- the problems you will face in your career -- can now be solved in a completely new way.

The Inbox Story:

And I saw it in my own inbox. After generative AI took off, the emails I received started looking the same. Same structure. Same tone. Same polished formatting.

But then I started getting full-page emails from students -- for simple things. Missing a class? One full page. Things that could be explained in two lines. AI made it easy to write more, but not necessarily better.

What if instead of using AI to produce more text, we taught students to use AI to analyze supply chains, build intelligent systems, automate engineering workflows? That gap is the reason this course exists.

I am Mohammad Dehghani -- Associate Teaching Professor at Northeastern, Department of Mechanical and Industrial Engineering. My research is in applied generative AI, simulation, and decision science. But more importantly for this course, I am a practitioner. I build with these tools every day. This course comes from that experience.

This course has fourteen modules organized in four phases, plus a midterm hackathon.

Phase One is Foundations. You start by connecting to AI models through code. Then you see what's possible with tools like Excel GPT. Then you learn to communicate with them precisely through prompt engineering.

Phase Two is Core. LangChain for chaining operations, RAG for grounding AI in real documents, and agents that reason and act autonomously.

At the midpoint, you put it all together in a hackathon.

Phase Three is Advanced. Multi-agent systems, building frontends, the Model Context Protocol, and vision.

Phase Four is Production. The latest SDKs, workflow automation, and your final project.

TA covers:

1. Course structure: 14 modules across 4 phases + midterm hackathon.

2. Labs: All labs run on Google Colab (free tier). No local setup needed.

3. Assignments: Submitted through Canvas.

4. Communication: Email with "DADS 5250" in the subject line. MS Teams for quick questions.

5. Participation & Discussion: Active participation expected. Discussion posts are part of your grade.

6. AI Policy: AI use is encouraged in this course. You must acknowledge when and how you used AI tools.

7. API Keys: You will need OpenAI and Gemini API keys. Keep them secret. Budget approximately $20-30 for the entire semester.

8. Office hours: Times and location posted on Canvas. Come prepared with specific questions.

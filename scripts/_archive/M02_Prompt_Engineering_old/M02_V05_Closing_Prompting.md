PART 1: RECAP
------------------------

Here is what changed this week.

Before this module, you could send a message to a model and get a response. Now you control how it thinks. Not just what it says, but the structure of its reasoning.

You learned the three-layer framework: system, user, assistant. You practiced the four elements: role, task, context, format. You applied zero-shot, few-shot, and chain-of-thought strategies and saw how each one shapes the output differently.

That is not prompting. That is engineering.

[ANIMATION: Quick visual recap — three-layer stack, four-element quadrant, three strategy icons — appearing in sequence]

PART 2: WHAT IS NEXT
------------------------

So far, the model gives you text. Paragraphs, bullet points, prose. That is useful, but it is not always what you need.

What if you need the model to give you JSON, not prose? What if you need it to return data in a format your application can parse automatically? What if you need it to call a function?

That is Module 3: Structured Output. We go from conversation to computation.

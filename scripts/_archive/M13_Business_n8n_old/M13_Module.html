Theory is over. This module is about deployment and business impact.

REVIEW -- First Draft

n8n is a workflow automation platform. Think of it as a visual programming environment where each node is an action -- send an email, call an API, query a database, process text with AI.

You connect nodes with wires. Data flows from one node to the next. When something triggers the workflow -- a new email, a scheduled time, a webhook -- the entire pipeline runs automatically.

What makes n8n special for us is its AI integration. You can drop an LLM node into any workflow. Receive a document, extract information with GPT, store it in a database, and send a summary email. All without writing a single line of code.

It is open-source, self-hostable, and has over 400 integrations. For our purposes, it is the fastest way to go from "I built an AI prototype" to "I deployed an AI system."

REVIEW -- First Draft

Now that you have the basics, let us connect LLMs to n8n workflows.

The pattern is simple. A trigger fires. Data comes in. An AI node processes it -- classifying, extracting, summarizing, or generating. The result flows to the next node -- database, email, Slack, spreadsheet.

I will show you three patterns. First, document processing -- a file arrives, AI extracts structured data, it goes into a database. Second, intelligent routing -- an email comes in, AI classifies it, and the workflow routes it to the right team. Third, content generation -- a scheduled trigger fires, AI generates a report from database data, and it gets emailed to stakeholders.

These are not toy examples. These are the exact patterns businesses use in production today.

GenAI in Business

Generative AI is being deployed across every major industry. Here are the patterns that work -- verified use cases with measurable business impact.

Finance -- Fraud Detection & Risk Analysis

Banks use LLMs to analyze transaction patterns and flag anomalies in real-time. JPMorgan's COiN system reviews commercial loan agreements in seconds -- work that previously took 360,000 hours annually. GenAI-powered chatbots handle 80%+ of routine customer inquiries at institutions like Bank of America (Erica).

Healthcare -- Triage & Documentation

AI triage systems analyze patient symptoms and route them to appropriate care levels. Ambient clinical documentation tools (like Nuance DAX) listen to doctor-patient conversations and generate structured notes automatically, saving physicians 2+ hours per day.

Manufacturing -- Quality Control

Vision AI inspects products on assembly lines faster and more consistently than human inspectors. BMW uses AI-powered visual inspection across its production lines. Defect detection rates improved by 30%+ while false positive rates dropped.

Marketing -- Content at Scale

Companies like Shopify and HubSpot have embedded GenAI directly into their platforms. Marketing teams generate ad copy, email sequences, and social media content at 10x the speed. The key -- human review remains essential for brand voice and accuracy.

Legal -- Contract Review

AI tools like Harvey (backed by Google, Sequoia) analyze contracts, identify risks, and draft amendments. Large law firms report 40-60% time savings on routine contract review. The technology does not replace lawyers -- it makes them dramatically more efficient.

AI Startup Success Stories

These companies went from zero to significant revenue by building focused AI products that solve specific problems.

Jasper AI
$1.5B valuation (2022) -- AI content generation

Built an AI writing assistant for marketing teams. Grew to $80M+ ARR in under 2 years. Key insight: they did not sell "AI" -- they sold "marketing content faster." Faced headwinds as ChatGPT commoditized text generation, but pivoted to enterprise marketing workflows.

Midjourney
Profitable from day one -- AI image generation

A team of ~40 people built one of the most popular AI image generators. No VC funding. Profitable from launch. Revenue estimated at $200M+ annually. Distributed entirely through Discord -- unconventional but effective.

Perplexity
$9B valuation (2024) -- AI-powered search

Challenged Google by building an AI search engine that gives direct answers with citations. Grew to 100M+ monthly users. Revenue from subscriptions ($20/month Pro) and enterprise. Founded by ex-Google AI researchers.

Cursor
Fastest-growing dev tool -- AI code editor

Built an AI-native code editor (fork of VS Code) that deeply integrates LLMs into the development workflow. Grew from 0 to 100K+ paying subscribers in months. Key insight: AI copilots work best when they understand your full codebase, not just the current file.

What They Have in Common

  • Specific problem: Each solves one thing well before expanding
  • Distribution advantage: Jasper via content marketing, Midjourney via Discord, Cursor via developer word-of-mouth
  • Speed: All shipped fast and iterated based on user feedback
  • AI as enabler, not product: They sell outcomes (better content, faster code), not "AI"

Platform Comparison -- When to Use What

n8n is not the only automation option. Here is how it compares to alternatives, and when each makes sense.

PlatformBest ForAI IntegrationCost
n8nTechnical teams, self-hosted, complex workflowsNative LLM nodes, custom API callsFree (self-hosted) / $20+/mo (cloud)
ZapierNon-technical users, simple automationsBuilt-in AI actions, limited customization$20+/mo
Custom CodeUnique requirements, full controlUnlimited -- you write itDeveloper time
Cloud FunctionsEvent-driven, serverless, high scaleVia API calls in codePay-per-execution

Decision Framework

  • Simple automation, non-technical team: Zapier
  • Complex workflows, technical team, data privacy: n8n (self-hosted)
  • Unique requirements or high scale: Custom code or cloud functions
  • Rapid prototyping, then production: Start with n8n, migrate to code if needed

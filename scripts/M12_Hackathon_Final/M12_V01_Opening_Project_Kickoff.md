PART 1: OPENING HOOK
------------------------

Twelve weeks ago, you made your first API call.

You did not know what a token was. You were not sure what temperature meant. You copied a code snippet from the lab, ran it, and when the model responded, it felt like magic.

Look at you now.

You build agents that reason through multi-step tasks. You orchestrate teams of specialized agents with CrewAI and LangGraph. You connect AI to any tool through MCP. You process images with vision APIs. You evaluate model performance with real metrics. You automate entire workflows without writing a single line of code.

Twelve weeks. That is all it took.

[ANIMATION: Timeline showing the journey -- M01 (first API call) through M11 (workflow automation), each module represented by a small icon, lighting up in sequence]

PART 2: WHAT MAKES A GREAT FINAL PROJECT
------------------------

Your final project is not an exam. It is not a homework assignment. It is a portfolio piece. It is the thing you show in a job interview when someone asks, "What can you build with AI?"

So let me tell you what makes a great one.

First, solve a real problem. Not a textbook exercise. Not a demo. A problem that someone actually has. It could be a problem at your job. A problem in your research. A problem in your daily life. The best projects start with "I wish I could..." and end with "Now I can."

Second, scope it right. You have two weeks. You are not building a startup. You are building a working prototype that demonstrates mastery. Pick one core feature and make it work beautifully. You can always add more later.

Third, use at least three techniques from this course. An API call plus prompting is Module 1. A final project should combine -- RAG plus agents plus evaluation. Or multi-agent plus MCP plus workflow automation. Show range.

[ANIMATION: Three cards appearing -- "Real Problem," "Right Scope," "Three Techniques" -- each with a brief icon]

PART 3: WHAT PAST STUDENTS HAVE BUILT
------------------------

Let me give you some examples to spark ideas.

One student built a customer support agent that reads ticket history from a database, classifies urgency, drafts a response, and routes complex cases to a human. She used RAG, agents, and guardrails. She got a job offer from the company she built it for.

Another student built a research assistant that takes a topic, searches academic papers, summarizes findings, identifies contradictions, and generates a literature review. Multi-agent with CrewAI. His advisor now uses it for every new project.

A third built a personal finance analyzer. Upload bank statements as images. Vision API extracts transactions. An agent categorizes spending and generates a weekly summary emailed through an n8n workflow.

Real problems. Real solutions. Portfolio-worthy.

[ANIMATION: Three project showcase cards with brief descriptions and technique badges]

PART 4: THE PRESENTATION
------------------------

You will present your project to the class. Five minutes. Live demo. No slides. Show the thing working.

The best presentations tell a story. "Here is the problem. Here is why it matters. Here is what I built. Here is it working. Here is what I learned."

PART 5: LET US BEGIN
------------------------

This project is your portfolio piece. Make it count.

You have the skills. You have the tools. You have two weeks. Start today.
